Your architecture strategy determines how your organization’s work is organized on the platform — from how you structure groups and projects to how you manage content and collaboration.

## What Is Architecture Strategy?

Architecture strategy is the deliberate planning of how your organization uses the platform’s features to best represent your work, enable collaboration, and achieve your goals.

## Key Decisions

### 1. Single Organization vs. Multiple Organizations

**Single Organization** Best for organizations with a unified structure. All projects, resources, and groups exist under one organizational profile.

**Multiple Organizations** Best for federated networks, large organizations with distinct divisions, or coalitions where member organizations maintain independent identities.

### 2. How to Structure Your Groups

Groups can represent:

- **Geographic chapters** – Regional or local affiliates
- **Program areas** – Different focus areas or initiatives
- **Working groups** – Temporary or permanent collaborative bodies
- **Partner networks** – Coalitions or alliances

### 3. Project Ownership

Decide whether projects are:

- **Organization-owned** – The organization controls the project
- **Group-owned** – A specific group within the org manages the project
- **Collaboratively owned** – Multiple organizations co-manage a project

### 4. Content and Resource Strategy

Plan how you’ll use [Resource Publishing](/platform-areas/resource-publishing/about):

- What types of resources will you publish?
- Who is your audience (internal, external, or both)?
- How frequently will you publish?
- Who is responsible for content quality?

## Recommended Patterns

### Pattern A: Simple Hierarchy

Ideal for small-to-medium organizations.

```text
Organization
├── Groups (by program area)
│   ├── Projects
│   └── Resources
└── Organization-wide Projects
```

### Pattern B: Geographic Network

Ideal for organizations with chapters or regional affiliates.

```text
National Organization
├── Regional Groups
│   ├── Local Chapters
│   └── Regional Projects
└── National Programs
```

### Pattern C: Coalition Model

Ideal for multi-organizational coalitions.

```text
Coalition Organization
├── Member Organizations (as groups)
├── Joint Projects
└── Shared Resources
```

## Getting Help

Not sure which architecture is right for you? Contact your platform administrator or reach out to support for a consultation.
