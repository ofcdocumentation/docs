Your organizational profile is the public face of your organization on the platform. A well-configured profile builds credibility, helps others find you, and enables effective collaboration.

## Why Your Organizational Profile Matters

A complete organizational profile:

- Makes your organization discoverable by potential partners and collaborators
- Communicates your mission and values to the community
- Serves as a central hub for your projects and resources
- Builds trust with other organizations and individuals

## Setting Up Your Profile

### 1. Create Your Organization

Navigate to **Organizations → Create New** and provide:

- **Organization Name** – Your official name as you want it displayed
- **Organization Type** – (e.g., nonprofit, coalition, company, government)
- **Website** – Your organization’s primary web presence

### 2. Complete Your Profile Information

Once created, fill in your full profile:

- **Mission Statement** – A concise description of your organization’s purpose
- **Description** – A longer narrative about your work and impact
- **Focus Areas** – Tags that describe your areas of work
- **Geographic Focus** – Where your work primarily takes place
- **Year Founded** – Optional, adds credibility

### 3. Upload Your Logo

Your logo appears across the platform wherever your organization is mentioned. Upload a high-quality image in PNG or SVG format.

<Tip>
  Logos display best at a 1:1 aspect ratio (square). Minimum recommended size is 400×400 pixels.
</Tip>

### 4. Add a Cover Image

A cover image appears at the top of your organization’s profile page. Use this space to showcase your work, brand, or community.

### 5. Add Contact Information

Help others reach you:

- Primary contact email
- Phone number (optional)
- Physical address (optional)
- Social media links

## Verifying Your Organization

Verification adds a badge to your profile, signaling authenticity. To apply for verification:

1. Complete all required profile fields
2. Navigate to **Settings → Verification**
3. Submit the verification request with supporting documentation

## Managing Profile Administrators

Multiple people can manage your organizational profile. See [Roles & Permissions](/organizational-profiles/roles-and-permissions) to learn how to add and manage admins.
