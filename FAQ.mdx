# **Account Setup**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I update my profile content (Personal account or Organizational account)?">
    To update the information on a profile, log in and click the icon next to your name or organization's name in the top right corner, select "Settings," and follow the tabs to update basic info, profile configurations, and contact information.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I set preferences for notifications?">
    You can check your notifications by clicking the bell icon next to your profile icon in the upper right corner. You will also receive email notifications for new discussions, tasks, and invitations from a dedicated platform email address.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I join groups or projects as an individual user?">
    There are three main ways: signing up through a customized invitation link sent by an Organization, being invited directly by another member, or searching the "Community" tab to find a group and clicking the "Join" button in its banner.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What do I use the feed tab for?">
    The feed is a dynamic space for real-time coordination and communication, displaying posts, calendar events, and attachments. Each group and project has a feed which all members of that space can view. On your personal profile, the feed aggregates information from all your connected spaces, showing assigned tasks and your personal to-do list.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if I create resources, discussion threads, projects, etc. from my personal account vs. my organizational account?">
    When posting from a personal account, content is visible to your connections or the members within the specific space you post it in. Organization accounts should be used to create projects and groups, add resources, and generate discussion threads; notably, only Organizational profiles can initiate new Discussion threads in projects or groups.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I get my profile, project, group, or resource to have a pin on the map view?">
    For a profile, project, or group, navigate to settings and enter Latitude and Longitude in the "Public Addresses" tab, using the "Get From Address" or “Use Map” tool to save a pin. For a resource, enter these coordinates in the "Contact Info" tab of the settings using the same tools.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I recover my password and user name?">
    If you need to recover your credentials or change your username, you must contact [support@openfuturecoalition.org](mailto:support@openfuturecoalition.org).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I connect with other people on the platform?">
    Navigate to the "Community" menu, click the "People" tab, find the member, and click "Connect" on their profile banner to send a request.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Projects**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What are the different ways I can use a project space?">
    Projects serve as structured environments for coordinating time-bound initiatives, collaborative workstreams, and measurable impact efforts. They provide an operational layer for managing multi-partner initiatives, delivering programs, tracking grant-funded projects, coordinating research, and documenting portfolios.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I invite collaborators to a project?">
    A member must first be a "Connection" of the sponsoring organization through an invitation link or direct request. Once connected, they can be added as collaborators via the "Preferences" tab in the "Edit Project" screen.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What features can I use to manage project coordination and communication?">
    You can use the feed for updates, discussions with tags, shared calendars for milestones, task assignments, and visual tools like Gantt charts and swimlanes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I assign managers to a project?">
    Assigning a manager is a two-step process: first, add the user to the Organization Manager list in the organization’s profile settings; then, select them from the "Project Managers" dropdown when creating or editing the project.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What functions does a project manager have?">
    Project managers have operational controls, including managing tasks for collaborators, creating and facilitating discussions, and adding calendar invites.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do managers of a project manage functions from their personal profile?">
    Managers can view and respond to assigned tasks and see upcoming calendar events directly from the Feed and Calendar tabs on their personal profile.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I manage tasks for a project?">
    In the "Management" tab, select "Add Task," choose a template, and fill in details like assignees and dates. To edit an existing task, click on it in the swimlane or Gantt view within the "Management" tab.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can see my project?">
    Visibility is determined by privacy settings, which can be set to Public, Private, Connections, or Custom.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I create a project as an individual instead of an Organization?">
    While possible from a personal profile, projects are intended to be created and managed by an organizational profile for maximum effectiveness.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Groups**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What are the different ways I can use a group space?">
    Groups are organized collaboration spaces for people with a shared purpose, ideal for efforts that are not time-bound or strictly task-focused. Examples include convening internal teams, regional collaboratives, learning cohorts, and hosting working groups within larger networks.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I invite collaborators to a group?">
    A member must first be a "Connection" of the sponsoring organization. Once connected, they can be added as collaborators via the "Preferences" tab in the "Edit Group" screen.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I join a group?">
    You can join via a sign-up invitation link, a direct email invitation, or by searching the Community and clicking "Join".
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What features can I use to manage group activities?">
    Groups include newsfeeds, discussion boards, calendars for scheduling meetings, and a group resource library.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can multiple groups sponsor a project?">
    Yes, multiple groups can sponsor a project by adding them in the "sponsoring group" field within the project settings.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I nest my groups and projects?">
    When setting up a project, select a "Sponsoring Group". The project will then be listed on that group's overview page, and its data will roll up to the sponsoring group.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can see my group?">
    Visibility is controlled by the "Show in Community" setting; "no" makes the group internal and invitation-only, while "yes" allows anyone on the platform to view and request to join.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I create a group as an individual instead of an Organization?">
    Groups are intended to be managed by an organizational profile, although creation from a personal profile is technically possible.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Calendars**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I set the date range for viewing calendar events?">
    In the calendar view, set a date range at the top to display events within those dates below. On a project or group page, it filters specifically for that space; on a personal profile, it shows an aggregate of all connected spaces.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I manage attendance of an event?">
    When creating an event, you can set a max capacity and a maximum guest count per invitee. All members of a space are automatically invited, but organizers can customize this using the privacy settings wheel next to the save button.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do calendar events sync with other calendars like Google?">
    Yes, by clicking the “add to calendar” icon in the group invite, you can download and save the event to your external calendar.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can I send calendar invitations to?">
    Project and Organization Managers can create shared events for members and collaborators. Individuals can also create personal events on their private profile calendar.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Privacy**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I determine who can see an attached file?">
    Files typically inherit the permissions of their "container." To change this retroactively, go to the "Files" tab on your individual profile, select "Settings" for the file, and use the “Files & Permissions” tab.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I determine who can see a resource I am publishing?">
    Privacy is set during creation in the "Files and Permissions" tab. To edit existing settings, go to "My Resources," select the resource, and use the gear icon to "Edit Privacy".
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I determine who can see an Impact Dashboard?">
    Dashboards can be public via URL or shared selectively. Privacy settings are edited via the gear icon at the bottom of the edit menu in the “Pages” section of your profile.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Attachments (Files)**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I use the files tab?">
    In projects and groups, the Files tab is view-only. To add, edit, or delete files, you must use the "Files" tab on your personal or organizational profile, accessed via your icon in the top right.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I attach a file to a post or discussion?">
    In the “Discussion” tab of a project or group, select “Add New” and click the attachment icon to drag and drop a file or select an existing one.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Resources**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I publish and share a resource?">
    Use the Resource Publishing tool to add info and set visibility, then use the "Publishing Wizard" to finalize which files are visible.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I view a resource?">
    Public resources are found in the Open Impact Resource Library. Space-specific resources are found in the "Resources" tab of the relevant group or project.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Pages/Dashboards**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I create a dashboard?">
    Select "Pages" from your profile menu, click "Add New Page," and use the "Start Creating" button for the drag-and-drop canvas.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I share a dashboard?">
    If set to public, you can share via URL. Set it to public in the privacy settings (gear icon) under the "Edit" menu of the specific page.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I add a dashboard to a project?">
    Navigate to the "Dashboard" tab in the target project, select your created page from the dropdown, and click "edit selected page" to update and connect it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I connect data to a dashboard?">
    Add a module (like a Chart) and choose a "Data Source Method" to connect to live data (API, Spreadsheet) or historical data (CSV, Manual Entry).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Project Management/Tasks**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I use the management tab to assign and manage tasks?">
    In the "Management" tab, click "Add Task," select a template, and luego choose the manager and collaborator group to set an assignee.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I view and respond to an assigned task?">
    On your personal profile, find the "Tasks" list in the Feed tab, select "Go" to open it, enter your data, and mark it complete.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I use project management for training curriculum?">
    Facilitators can guide participants through tasks and stages. Use “Bulk Assign” to give a copy of a task (like a module) to every assignee.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can assign or manage tasks on behalf of a project?">
    Task management is restricted to the Project Creator (Admin) and designated Project Managers.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I use swimlanes and Gantt charts?">
    Toggle between these views in the "Management" tab. Gantt charts visualize sequences and durations, while swimlanes visualize tasks by status; both allow for drag-and-drop updates.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Forms**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I use a form to report on measurable results?">
    Use a "Measurable Result" task template, which allows an assignee to enter numerical data that a manager can then approve for visualization on a dashboard.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
