# **Account Setup**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I update my profile content (Personal account or Organizational account)?">
    To update the information on a profile, log in and click the icon next to your name or organization's name in the top right corner, select "Settings," and follow the tabs to update basic info, profile configurations, and contact information.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I set preferences for notifications?">
    You can check your notifications by clicking the bell icon next to your profile icon in the upper right corner. You will also receive email notifications for new discussions, tasks, and invitations from a dedicated platform email address.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I join groups or projects as an individual user?">
    There are three main ways: signing up through a customized invitation link sent by an Organization, being invited directly by another member, or searching the "Community" tab to find a group and clicking the "Join" button in its banner.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What do I use the feed tab for?">
    The feed is a dynamic space for real-time coordination and communication, displaying posts, calendar events, and attachments. Each group and project has a feed which all members of that space can view. On your personal profile, the feed aggregates information from all your connected spaces, showing assigned tasks and your personal to-do list.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if I create resources, discussion threads, projects, etc. from my personal account vs. my organizational account?">
    When posting from a personal account, content is visible to your connections or the members within the specific space you post it in. Organization accounts should be used to create projects and groups, add resources, and generate discussion threads; notably, only Organizational profiles can initiate new Discussion threads in projects or groups.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I get my profile, project, group, or resource to have a pin on the map view?">
    For a profile, project, or group, navigate to settings and enter Latitude and Longitude in the "Public Addresses" tab, using the "Get From Address" or “Use Map” tool to save a pin. 

    For a resource, enter these coordinates in the "Contact Info" tab of the settings using the same tools.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I recover my password and user name?">
    If you need to recover your credentials or change your username, you must contact [support@openfuturecoalition.org](mailto:support@openfuturecoalition.org).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I connect with other people on the platform?">
    Navigate to the "Community" menu, click the "People" tab, find the member, and click "Connect" on their profile banner to send a request.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Projects**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What are the different ways I can use a project space?">
    Projects on the Open Impact Platform serve as structured environments for coordinating time-bound initiatives, collaborative workstreams, and measurable impact efforts. While Groups organize people around a shared context, Projects provide the operational layer where teams plan, implement, and track specific initiatives, deliverables, and outcomes. This includes:

    - Managing multi-partner initiatives
    - Delivering structured programs or learning cohorts
    - Tracking grant-funded projects and reporting outcomes
    - Coordinating research or field-building initiatives
    - Documenting and showcasing project portfolios
    - Supporting community-based or participatory projects
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I invite collaborators to a project?">
    A member must first be a "Connection" of the sponsoring organization through an invitation link or direct request. Once connected, they can be added as collaborators via the "Preferences" tab in the "Edit Project" screen.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What features can I use to manage project coordination and communication?">
    You can use the feed for updates, discussions with tags for ongoing dialogue, shared calendars for milestones, assign tasks, and visual management tools like Gantt charts and swimlanes for task implementation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I assign managers to a project?">
    Assigning a manager is a two-step process: first, add the user to the Organization Manager list in the organization’s profile settings; then, select them from the "Project Managers" dropdown when creating or editing the project.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What functions does a project manager have?">
    Project managers have operational controls, including managing tasks for collaborators, creating and facilitating discussions, and adding calendar invites.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do managers of a project manage functions from their personal profile?">
    Managers can view and respond to assigned tasks and see upcoming calendar events directly from the Feed and Calendar tabs on their personal profile.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I manage tasks for a project?">
    In the "Management" tab, select "Add Task," choose a template, and fill in details like assignees and dates. To edit an existing task, click on it in the swimlane or Gantt view within the "Management" tab.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can see my project?">
    Visibility is determined by privacy settings, which can be set to Public, Private, Connections, or Custom.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I create a project as an individual instead of an Organization?">
    While possible from a personal profile, projects are intended to be created and managed by an organizational profile for maximum effectiveness.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Groups**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What are the different ways I can use a group space?">
    Groups on the Open Impact Platform serve as organized collaboration spaces that bring people together around a shared purpose or context. Designed to support both coordination and knowledge exchange, Groups create a structured environment where members can communicate, collaborate, and access shared resources.

    Groups are best for efforts that aren’t going to be time bound, task focused, and measurable. Groups may have one or more Projects that they sponsor. We suggest starting with an initial aggregate Group to create gravity of participation as you build your network engagement and then create additional Groups or Projects as they are needed. Some examples include:

    - Convening internal teams and distributed workspaces
    - Organizing cross-organizational affinity groups or communities of practice
    - Coordinating regional or place-based collaboratives
    - Hosting learning cohorts, fellowships, or program communities
    - Engaging event participants before, during, and after gatherings
    - Facilitating working groups within larger networks or coalitions
    - Creating ongoing spaces for dialogue and shared learning
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I invite collaborators to a group?">
    A member must first be a "Connection" of the sponsoring organization, either by using an invitation link or a direct connection request. Once connected, they can be added as collaborators via the "Preferences" tab in the "Edit Group" screen.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I join a group?">
    You can join via a sign-up invitation link, a direct email invitation, or by searching for the group in the Community and clicking "Join".
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What features can I use to manage group activities?">
    Groups include shared newsfeeds, discussion boards, calendars for scheduling meetings, and a group resource library.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can multiple groups sponsor a project?">
    Yes, multiple groups can sponsor a project. To add a sponsoring group to a project, navigate to the settings of the project and add a group in the sponsoring group field.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I nest my groups and projects?">
    When setting up a project, you can select a "Sponsoring Group". The project will then be listed on that group's overview page and its data will roll up to the sponsoring group.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can see my group?">
    When setting up the group, you can control who sees it. This is determined by the "Show in Community" setting; selecting "no" makes the group internal and invitation-only. Selecting “yes” means anyone on the Open Impact platform can view the group and request to join.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I create a group as an individual instead of an Organization?">
    While it is possible to create a  group from a personal profile, groups are intended to be created and managed by an organizational profile and are most effective when linked to a managing organization.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Calendars**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I set the date range for viewing calendar events?">
    In the calendar view in the feed of your profile, group, or project, set a date range at the top of the calendar. This will then display the calendar events within those dates below.

    When viewing a project or group, the calendar automatically filters to show only the events relevant to that space. On your personal profile, the calendar displays an aggregate of events from all connected groups and projects.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I manage attendance of an event?">
    When creating a calendar event, you can set the max capacity (total maximum number of attendees) and Maximum Guest Count (Maximum guests each invitee can bring). All members of a group or project are automatically invited to the calendar event. If an organizer wants to customize who receives the invite, they can use the wheel next to save to select privacy settings.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do calendar events sync with other calendars like Google?">
    Yes! When you view the calendar invite in your group you’ll see an icon to “add to calendar”. This will prompt you to save the invite. When you click the downloaded you will be prompted to “add new event to your calendar”- select “ok”.** **
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can I send calendar invitations to?">
    Project Managers and Organization Managers can create shared events for the members and collaborators of a project or group. You can also create personal events on your private profile calendar.  All members of a group or project are automatically invited to the calendar event. If an organizer wants to customize who receives the invite, they can use the wheel next to save to select privacy settings.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Privacy**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I determine who can see an attached file?">
    Files typically inherit the permissions of their "container." To change this retroactively, go to the "Files" tab on your individual profile, select "Settings" for the file, and use the “Files & Permissions” tab.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I determine who can see a resource I am publishing?">
    Privacy is set in the "Files and Permissions" tab during the resource creation process, with options for Public, Private, Connections, or Custom.

    To view or edit the privacy settings of a resource, click on the “My Resources” tab at the top of the platform and select the resource you are interested in. Once you are on that resource’s page, click the gear icon in the banner and then “Edit Privacy”. 
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I determine who can see an Impact Dashboard?">
    Dashboards can be made public via a URL, or shared selectively by attaching them to specific platform areas like projects. When creating or updating a dashboard (navigate to this area by selecting your icon in the top right, then “pages”), you can edit the privacy settings by clicking on the gear icon at the bottom of the edit menu. 
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Attachments (Files)**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I use the files tab?">
    In projects and groups, the Files tab is a view-only repository of media shared in that space. To add, edit, or delete files, you must use the "Files" tab on your personal or organizational profile. (To get there, click on your icon in the top right corner, then “Files”).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I attach a file to a post or discussion?">
    To create a discussion, navigate to the “Discussion” tab in a project or group. Select “Add New”. To attach a file, click the attachment icon and add from existing files or drag and drop a new file.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Resources**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I publish and share a resource?">
    Use the Resource Publishing tool to add basic info, set visibility, and attach files. After submitting, you must use the "Publishing Wizard" to finalize which files are visible.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I view a resource?">

# **Pages/Dashboards**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I create a dashboard?">
    Select "Pages" from your profile menu, click "Add New Page," and use the "Start Creating" button for the drag-and-drop canvas.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I share a dashboard?">
    If the dashboard is set to public, you can share the page through a URL.

    To set the dashboard to public:

    - Navigate to the edit the dashboard (To do this, select your icon in the top right, then “pages”, then select “Edit” under the gear next to the dashboard you want to work with)
    - Click on the gear icon at the bottom of the edit menu, this will open the the privacy settings.
    - Under “Select Privacy”, click “Public”, then “Add”
    - Click “Update Page”

    If you are having trouble generating the URL, make sure the dashboard is set to public. You may need to delete any previously assigned “Private” security types.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I add a dashboard to a project?">
    To  share a dashboard to a project space so that members of that space can view it:

    - Go to the project you want to connect dashboard and navigate to the “Dashboard” tab
    - Select the page you created in dropdown (next to edit selected page)
    - Click “edit selected page” which will open the dashboard in a new tab and click “Update Page”
    - Return to the dashboard tab to view the updated dashboard page. You may need to refresh the page
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I connect data to a dashboard?">
    Add a module (like a Chart) and choose a "Data Source Method" to connect to live data (API, Spreadsheet) or historical data (CSV, Manual Entry).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

# **Project Management/Tasks**

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I use the management tab to assign and manage tasks?">
    Go to the "Management" tab, click "Add Task," select a template, and enter the task details. On the next screen, select the manager and the collaborator group to choose an assignee.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I view and respond to an assigned task?">
    Log into your personal profile, find the "Tasks" list on your Feed tab, and select "Go" to open the task, enter data, add attachments, and mark it complete when you are ready.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I use project management for training curriculum?">
    Project spaces allow facilitators to deliver curriculum by guiding participants through structured tasks and defined stages. When assigning tasks, such as an assignment or module, use “Bulk Assign” to assign a copy of a task that each assignee must complete.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Who can assign or manage tasks on behalf of a project?">
    Task management is performed by the Project Creator (Admin) and designated Project Managers.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I use swimlanes and Gantt charts?">
    View Gantt charts and Swimlanes under the "Management" tab of a project. You can toggle between the two views in the top right of the tab. 

    Gantt charts are used to visualize task sequences and durations; you can drag task bars to update dates. Swimlanes visualize tasks by status; you can drag cards between columns to update their status.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
