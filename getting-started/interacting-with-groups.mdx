Groups bring together people with shared interests, missions, or affiliations. This guide explains how to find, join, and participate in groups.

## What Are Groups?

Groups are communities built around a shared purpose — they can be organizations, coalitions, interest communities, or any other collective. Groups may sponsor projects, publish resources, and connect members.

## Finding Groups

### Browse the Groups Directory

Go to **Platform Areas → Groups** to explore all groups on the platform. Filter by:

- Topic or focus area
- Organization type
- Location
- Size

### Discover Through Projects

Many projects are affiliated with groups. When viewing a project, you’ll see its associated group(s) listed on the project page.

## Joining a Group

1. Open a group’s profile page
2. Review the group’s mission, members, and projects
3. Click **Join Group**
4. For closed groups, submit a request and wait for approval from a group admin

<Tip>
  Check a group’s membership settings before requesting to join. Open groups allow anyone to become a member instantly.
</Tip>

## Participating in a Group

As a group member, you can:

- **Engage with group content** on the activity feed
- **Access group resources** and shared files
- **Collaborate on group projects**
- **Connect with other members** in the group

## Group Roles

Different roles carry different permissions within a group:

| Role | Capabilities |
| --- | --- |
| **Member** | View content, participate in discussions |
| **Contributor** | Upload files, create projects |
| **Moderator** | Manage content, approve members |
| **Admin** | Full group management, roles, settings |

See [Roles & Permissions](/organizational-profiles/roles-and-permissions) for a complete breakdown.
