# Setting up your profile

Your profile is your identity on the platform. This guide walks you through setting up your profile so you can connect, collaborate, and get the most out of your experience.

## Joining the Platform

- Set up your personal account by signing up through a customized invite link sent to you by an Organization. 
- ​​When you use the invitation link, you will see a button in the upper right to 'Sign Up’. 
- Once you’ve created your account you will automatically be signed in.

# Navigating the Platform

## Your Profile

On your personal profile you will also have tabs for “Feed”, “Discussion”, “Connections”, Resources, etc. These are the aggregates of activities and communications from the various Groups or Projects that you belong to, so you can view all the activities connected to your Groups and Project in one place.

| Overview Tab | View your public profile information (See ‘Update personal profile’ to edit this information) |
| :-- | :-- |
| Feed Tab | <ul><li>Make comments on posts from your groups or projects</li><li>View tasks that have been assigned from projects you are participating in</li><li>View a calendar of events from projects or groups you are a part of</li><li>Add and check off personal to-do list items</li></ul> |
| Discussion Tab | <ul><li>Create a personal discussion thread between yourself and your connections.</li><li>Search for and view a topical discussion and add comments</li></ul> |
| Projects Tab | View and filter the projects and groups you are a part of |
| Connections Tab | View and filter the People and organizations you are connected with _\*Learn how to connect with other members (Link to connect with members section)_ |

## Groups and Projects

In the top header of the platform, you will see tabs for “My Projects” and “My Groups”. Once you have joined a group or project, you will see them reflected when you click into those tabs.

### Joining

**Groups** are collaborative spaces for coordinating and communicating with members.

There are 3 ways you can join a Group:

1. If you were sent an invitation link to sign up for an account and join a Group, when you create your account, your request to join will be sent to the organization. Once it’s accepted you will receive a notification and see the Group in your “Groups” tab.
2. If you already have a profile on Open Impact and have been invited directly to join a Group, you will get an email notification that guides you to “Click to Join” 
3. The third way is to search the “Community” tab when logged in to your account, find a Group, open their profile, and select the “Join” button in the banner of the Group. The Group will then need to “accept” your request for you to join the Group. Once it’s accepted you will receive a notification and see the Group in your “Groups” tab.

**Projects** are for time-bound, task-focused, and measurable collaborative efforts.

To Join a Project: A manager of a Project sponsored by a Group that you are a member of can directly add you as a collaborator to a project. 

### Interacting with Groups and Projects

Once you have joined a Group or Project, you can visit the Group or Project “Feed” tab to post or comment, see Calendar events, and connect with collaborators. You can go to “Discussions” to add to a Discussion thread or “Resources” tab to view resources.

| Feed | The “Feed” tab shows posts, calendar events, and collaborators associated with the project or group. For projects the ‘Feed’ tab is also where collaborators engage in assigned tasks. You can view or rsvp to a calendar invitation, create a post to share with other members of the group or project or comment on posts from other members. Posts can include images and links. \_Note: Posts in the“Feed” are useful for more time sensitive conversations, sharing updates, sharing a link, etc. For ongoing topics of discussion that are organized and tagged for searchability, use the “Discussions” tab. \_ |
| :-- | :-- |
| Discussions | The “Discussions” tab is used for ongoing topics of discussion within a group or project. You can comment on a discussion and search via tags or keywords. Only Community Managers can create a new “Discussion” topic for a Group or Project. You can create a Discussion in your own personal profile and add “Connections” to join your Discussion, but it will only be visible to the connections you invited- not on a group or project level |

## Check Notifications

Next to your icon on your personal profile, in the upper right corner, you will see a bell \[Include bell icon\] that indicates when you have a new notification and what activity has happened. You will also receive email notifications for new discussions, new tasks, new invitations, etc. Email notifications will come from the email [donotreply@openfuturecoalition.org](mailto:donotreply@openfuturecoalition.org).
