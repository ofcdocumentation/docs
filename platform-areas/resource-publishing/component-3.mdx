Understanding how your resources are used helps you measure impact and improve future content.

## Resource Analytics

Every published resource includes an analytics dashboard showing:

- **Views** – How many times the resource page was viewed
- **Downloads** – How many times the file was downloaded (for file-based resources)
- **Shares** – How many times the resource was shared
- **Saves** – How many users have bookmarked it
- **Comments** – Number and content of comments

### Accessing Analytics

1. Navigate to your resource
2. Click **Analytics** in the top menu
3. View data for different time periods using the date picker

## Analytics Breakdown

### Audience

- **Organization types** – What types of organizations are accessing your resource
- **Geographic distribution** – Where viewers are located
- **Referral sources** – How people found your resource (search, direct link, social share, etc.)

### Engagement Over Time

See a timeline of views, downloads, and engagement. Identify spikes that correlate with promotions, events, or mentions.

### Comparative Performance

Compare this resource’s performance to your other resources or to similar resources in the same category.

## Exporting Analytics

Download your analytics data:

1. In the Analytics tab, click **Export**
2. Choose CSV or PDF format
3. Select the date range
4. Download your report

## Engaging With Your Audience

### Comments

Viewers can comment on your resource. Responding to comments builds community and can spark valuable conversations.

1. Navigate to your resource
2. Scroll to the **Comments** section
3. Click **Reply** to respond to a comment

### Moderating Comments

As the resource publisher, you can:

- **Hide** inappropriate comments
- **Delete** spam or abusive comments
- **Turn off comments** entirely (from Resource Settings)

### Featured in Collections

If your resource gains traction, it may be featured in community-curated collections or highlighted in the platform newsletter. Keep your metadata current to maximize these opportunities.

## Updating Based on Feedback

Use comments and analytics to improve your resources:

- If download rates are low despite high views, improve the description or check that the file opens correctly
- If viewers are dropping off, consider breaking a large resource into smaller pieces
- Respond to common questions in comments by updating the resource description
