Metrics and KPI components are the building blocks of your Impact Dashboard. They display a single key number with optional context like trends, targets, and comparisons.

## What Are Metric Components?

Metric components display quantitative indicators that matter to your work. Examples include:

- Number of people served
- Funds raised or deployed
- Volunteers engaged
- Policies influenced
- Resources published

## Component Types

### Simple Metric Card

Displays a single value with optional subtitle and trend indicator.

**Best for:** High-level KPIs you want to highlight prominently.

**Configuration options:**

- Value label and units
- Comparison period (vs. last month, last year, etc.)
- Trend direction indicator
- Color coding (positive/negative)

### Progress Bar

Shows progress toward a goal or target.

**Best for:** Fundraising thermometers, campaign goals, milestones.

**Configuration options:**

- Current value
- Target value
- Label format
- Color theme

### Metric Over Time (Sparkline)

A compact line chart showing how a metric has changed over time.

**Best for:** Showing momentum and trends alongside a current value.

**Configuration options:**

- Date range
- Aggregation period (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Trend line

## Setting Up a Metric Component

1. In your dashboard, click **\+ Add Component**
2. Select your preferred metric type
3. Enter a title for the component
4. Connect your data source (manual entry, platform data, or import)
5. Configure display options
6. Click **Save**

## Data Sources for Metrics

Metric components can pull from:

- **Manual input** – Enter numbers directly
- **Project data** – Auto-calculated from project activity
- **Imported data** – From a CSV/Excel spreadsheet
- **Connected integrations** – From third-party tools (if configured)

## Tips for Effective Metrics

<Tip>
  Choose metrics that directly reflect your theory of change. Avoid vanity metrics that look good but don’t reflect real impact.
</Tip>

- **Fewer is more** – Highlight 3-5 key metrics rather than overwhelming viewers
- **Context matters** – Always show a comparison (vs. goal, vs. last period)
- **Tell a story** – Arrange metrics to guide the viewer through your narrative
