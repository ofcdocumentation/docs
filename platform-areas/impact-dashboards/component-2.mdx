Data visualization components transform your numbers into charts, graphs, and visual stories that are easy to understand at a glance.

## Available Chart Types

### Line Chart

Shows change over time. Best for displaying trends across a continuous period.

**Use cases:** Monthly program reach, year-over-year growth, tracking progress over a campaign.

### Bar Chart

Compares values across categories. Can be displayed vertically or horizontally.

**Use cases:** Comparing impact across regions, showing program breakdown, before/after comparisons.

### Stacked Bar Chart

Like a bar chart, but shows how subcategories contribute to the total.

**Use cases:** Demographic breakdowns, funding by source, program mix over time.

### Pie / Donut Chart

Shows how a whole is divided into parts. Best when you have fewer than 6-7 categories.

**Use cases:** Budget allocation, audience demographics, resource type breakdown.

### Area Chart

Like a line chart, but fills the area below the line. Good for showing cumulative totals.

**Use cases:** Cumulative beneficiaries served, total funds raised over time.

### Scatter Plot

Shows the relationship between two variables.

**Use cases:** Correlating program investment with outcomes, comparing organizations.

## Configuring a Chart Component

1. Click **\+ Add Component** on your dashboard
2. Select your chart type
3. Connect your data source
4. Configure your axes:
   - **X-axis** – Usually time or category
   - **Y-axis** – Usually your measured value
5. Set labels, colors, and display options
6. Click **Save**

## Styling Your Charts

### Color Themes

Choose from preset color themes or create a custom palette that matches your branding. Go to **Dashboard Settings → Theme** to set a default for the entire dashboard.

### Labels and Annotations

- Add data labels directly on chart elements
- Create text annotations to highlight specific points
- Add reference lines to show targets or averages

### Legends

Toggle legends on or off. Customize legend placement (top, bottom, right, left).

## Combining Multiple Data Series

Most chart types support multiple data series. For example, you can show both a current year and prior year line on a single line chart.

1. In the chart configuration, click **\+ Add Series**
2. Connect the second data source
3. Choose a display style for the new series
