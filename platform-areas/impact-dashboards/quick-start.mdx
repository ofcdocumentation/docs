Dashboards allow you to integrate data from multiple sources, insert custom charts, and visual elements such as images and videos. They can be shared or embedded on external platforms and can be created at the level of a single project or aggregated across multiple projects and sites, allowing organizations to analyze performance and outcomes across distributed initiatives.

</Check>

## Before You Begin

You’ll need:

- An account on the platform
- Membership in at least one project or group
- Some data to display (you can enter it manually or connect a data source)

## Step 1: Create a New Dashboard

1. Navigate to **Platform Areas → Impact Dashboards**
2. Click **\+ New Dashboard**
3. Give your dashboard a name and optional description
4. Choose who can see it (public, platform members, or private)
5. Click **Create**

## Step 2: Add Your First Component

You’ll land on an empty dashboard canvas. Click **\+ Add Component** to add your first visualization.

Choose a component type:

- **Metric Card** – Display a single key number
- **Line Chart** – Show change over time
- **Bar Chart** – Compare values across categories
- **Pie Chart** – Show proportional breakdown
- **Map** – Display geographic data
- **Table** – Show detailed tabular data

## Step 3: Connect Your Data

After choosing a component type, you’ll configure its data source:

**Manual Entry** Enter data directly into the component. Good for one-time reports or data that doesn’t change frequently.

**Connect to Platform Data** Pull in data automatically from your projects or groups:

1. Select **Platform Data** as the source
2. Choose the project or group
3. Select the metric you want to track

**Import from Spreadsheet** Upload a CSV or Excel file:

1. Select **Import File**
2. Upload your file
3. Map the columns to the appropriate fields

## Step 4: Customize the Visualization

Once your data is connected, customize how it looks:

- **Title** – Name for this specific component
- **Color scheme** – Match your branding
- **Date range** – Filter to a specific time period
- **Display options** – Show/hide legends, labels, etc.

## Step 5: Arrange Your Dashboard

Drag and drop components to arrange them on the canvas. Resize components by dragging their corners.

## Step 6: Publish and Share

1. Click **Save** to save your progress
2. When ready, click **Publish**
3. Share the dashboard URL with your team or stakeholders

<Tip>
  Dashboards can be embedded on external websites using the embed code found in **Share → Embed**.
</Tip>
